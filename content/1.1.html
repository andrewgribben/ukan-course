1.1 Describing the data environment

The process of anonymising data relies on a careful consideration of the data environment. The data environment is, in a broad sense, the context. This could be people who are interested in the data, other related datasets, or processes that interact with the data.

For example, you release an open dataset on expenses. Then, considerations of the data environment are at least

  • all people who have access to the internet,
  • journalists who are are looking for a story on transparency,
  • expense datasets from previous years,
  • the internal people who access the dataset before it is anonymised,

and so forth.
